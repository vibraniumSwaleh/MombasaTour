Top 6 Beaches in Mombasa

March 13, 2016September 6, 2016 - by Steve - Leave a Comment

Mombasa is known to have the best beaches in Africa and the entire globe. Every year, thousands of holiday makers from different parts of the world throng the coastal Kenyan strip to enjoy the aura of white sandy beaches and exceptional weather. Mombasa is undoubtedly blessed when it comes to beach attractions; the long stretches of the beach, endless palm trees and cool breezes.

Anyone who comes to the Kenyan Coast is always keen to get a first-hand experience of the top beaches in Mombasa. You can never go short of things to do while visiting Mombasa beaches. Swimming, beach sports, diving, snorkeling as well as boat rides with transparent glasses at the bottom that allow you to see the rich marine life below are common; the experience is spectacular.

In this article, I will share with you the top 7 Mombasa beaches that are a must visit. The good news is that these beaches aren’t far apart and therefore, feel free move around and visit if you have time; I can assure you, every beach has its own unique characteristics and experiences.




1. Diani Beach

Lying on the Indian Ocean approximately 30km south of Mombasa city, Diani beach is the most popular beach associated with plenty of sunlight that allows you to indulge and experience tranquility that you will not find anywhere else.

In fact, Diani Beach in 2014 emerged top as Africa’s Leading Beach Destination in World Travel Awards. For those who’ve been here, you’ll agree with me that Diani Beach has a special appeal and charm; when you’re on the beach, you want to remain there and not leave at all.

Numerous world class beach resorts, widespread palm vegetation, giant coral reefs and a vibrant marine life put Diani Beach in its own class. While here, you can simply relax on the beach and unwind, take a walk, participate in beach sports or enjoy an afternoon picnic.

To get to Diani Beach, you have to cross the Likoni ferry crossing that connects with Mombasa Island. You can get here with private car or fly to the nearby Ukunda airstrip (there are direct flights from Kenya’s capital city, Nairobi). Alternatively, if you’re using public means, take public transport vehicles to Likoni ferry, alight and board the ferry to cross the channel and access the southern mainland.

Once you’re on the Likoni side, board a matatu that goes to Diani; they’re plenty of them. The ferry headaches are set to end soon as the Kenyan government has began constructing a 17.5km Dongo Kundu bypass that will provide a direct link to the South Coast from the western side of Mombasa city.

Images courtesy of  The Sands at Nomad

2. Nyali Beach

If you’re looking for a haven of tranquility that is not far away from the city and amenities, Nyali beach should be your ultimate destination. Located about 5km from Mombasa CBD and accessed via Links Road, this beach is very near town and is easily accessible using a taxi, tuktuk or Nyali Reef Matatus that begin their journey on Jomo Kenyatta Avenue near Nawal Center.

Located in the upmarket neighborhood of Nyali, the easy accessibility makes this beach rank highly amongst the top beaches in Mombasa.

The good thing about Nyali beach is that it’s pristine, calm and quiet. Apparently, the crowds here are relatively few especially if you want to be on a laid back beach that allows privacy. The beauty is outstanding; there are hundreds of palm trees and plenty of beach sports.

You can do anything here; relax and gaze at the sun, swim, sunbathe or surf. This is an ideal beach if you have kids; since it’s not overcrowded, you can easily keep an eye on them as they enjoy the water and sand.

Several top notch beach hotels are located on the shores. Also, there are several guesthouses and lodges in Nyali; most of them are within a walking distance and if you have to use a car or rickshaw, its less than 3 minutes drive to the beach.

Furthermore, there are large shopping malls nearby where you can quickly grab your snacks or swimwear before heading to the beach.

3. Bamburi Beach

Located about 10 km from Mombasa city centre, Bamburi beach has several beachfront hotels, apartments and villas. The famous ‘Pirates’ (Jomo Kenyatta Public beach) which is Mombasa’s and Kenya’s largest public beach is located on this stretch of Bamburi beach.

The Mombasa Marine Park is a major attraction here for those who’re fascinated with Marine life. Being the busiest public beach, there are several stalls where you can buy artifacts, traditional wear and snacks to enjoy on the beach. A lot of beach parties (Christmas and New Year parties) also take place on Bamburi beach.

If you love an active and lively beach; Pirates is a good option. You can relax, swim, catch up with friends or participate in beach and water sports, the beach fronts facing hotels are less busy and quiet as most people tend to remain on the pirates stretch.

You can easily get here with a private car, taxi, tuktuk or matatu. If you’re using public means, its best to board Mtwapa matatus from Digo Road in the CBD and alight at Pirates – it’s a major stage. Alternatively, if you’re visiting the beach resorts, specify which hotel you’re going to avoid getting lost.

You can also take Bamburi Mtambo matatus from Digo Road; but you will have to alight at the Bamburi cement junction and walk up about 200 meters to access the road to the beach.

4. Shanzu Beach

After Bamburi beach, the next beach further up is Shazu beach. Top world class resorts are located in the posh neighborhood of Shanzu that is well known to be peaceful and quiet. Shanzu beach is located approximately 14km from Mombasa CBD and has on numerous occasions, won the hearts of many as one of the top beaches in Mombasa.

Hotels on this stretch are closely packed together; there are also plenty of bars and relaxation spots where you can have some time off to reflect and indulge with nature.

The beachfronts are exquisite, quiet and relaxing. When you’re on Shanzu beach, make the most of your time because it’s one place you’ll not want to leave in a hurry. Accessibility is easy through Serena road that is adjacent to Shanzu Teachers College.

The road going in is tarmacked and has plenty of taxis, tuktuks and motorbikes (for those of you who love motorbike rides). From town, pick Mtwapa matatus from Digo Road; it’s about 15-20 mins from town depending on traffic.

5. Tiwi Beach

Tiwi is a quiet beach tucked away in Kwale County approximately 18km from Mombasa City. If you are tired, stressed and want absolute silence, this is the place to be.

For holidaymakers who simply want to unwind and keep it low; there are numerous good hotels on this stretch of the beach that incorporates a countryside feel.

I have realized a lot of older travelers love Tiwi beach thanks to its unique natural landscape that rejuvenates the mind and body. If you feel the north coast beaches are too fast and active for you, try Tiwi beach; you’ll not regret it.

 6. Copacabana Beach, Mtwapa

Mtwapa is a famous town located 16km north of Mombasa city famous for its vibrant nightlife and party scene. Mtwapa is a tourist magnet suburb loved by both foreign and local tourists.

I have realized that Mtwapa is associated so much with partying that people forget it also has a nice and quiet beach where you can sit and relax. From the beach, you get the chance to enjoy exotic views of the Indian Ocean and the Mtwapa creek.

There are numerous villas, private residences, restaurants and bars where you can go and have fun. Furthermore, Mtwapa has everything; good accommodation, shopping, banking facilities, entertainment and dining.

Images courtsey of Luck Apartments Facebook page 

 

The above are 6 top beaches in Mombasa. There are several smaller ones that we shall be discussing in future posts. They’re all great places to be and every beach has its own selling points. I guess you need to go on a tour and identify one that pleases you the most.

Have fun as you tour the top beaches in Mombasa. Please, don’t forget to share your experiences with us.

So, which is your favorite Mombasa beach? We’d love to hear from you

Enjoy your Mombasa beach vacation
